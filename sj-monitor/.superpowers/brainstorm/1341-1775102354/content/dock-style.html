A — Bottom Floating Dock

iOS/macOS inspired. Floats above content at the bottom. Active item has filled pill background. On desktop, stays at the bottom of the viewport. Clean pill shape with blur effect.

Pros

  • Thumb-friendly on mobile
  • Always visible, never scrolls away
  • Modern, premium feel

Cons

  • Takes ~60px of screen real estate
  • Needs pb-20 on content

B — Responsive Hybrid (Bottom on Mobile, Top Tabs on Desktop)

Bottom floating dock on mobile (≤768px). Reverts to top tab bar on desktop (≥768px). Best of both worlds — ergonomic on phone, spacious on laptop.

Pros

  • Optimized per device type
  • Desktop keeps full label width
  • Natural thumb zone on mobile

Cons

  • Two navigation modes to maintain
  • Slightly more complex CSS

C — Dark Floating Pill (macOS-style)

Center-anchored dark translucent pill dock. Bold, modern, premium feel. Works great on both mobile and desktop without changing style. Icons float above content at all times.

Pros

  • Very distinctive & premium
  • Same design on all screen sizes
  • High contrast — easy to find

Cons

  • Dark pill may feel heavy
  • Center anchor less ergonomic on large phones
